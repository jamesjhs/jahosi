👋 Welcome to splash!

splash! is a friendly planning tool for estimating how a small outdoor playpool warms up, cools down overnight, and what it may cost to hold a target temperature. You can type your own 3-day forecast manually at any time, or, when you are online in the UK, use a postcode to auto-fill the next three days from free public weather services.

The planner blends pool size, heater output, tariffs, passive cover use, current water temperature, and short-term weather into one place so you can compare likely outcomes before spending money or time.

  • Manual forecast entry always stays available, even after postcode auto-fill.
  • Set pool season start and end dates to see a cost breakdown for your actual outdoor season, including water fill costs (UK Anglian Water metered baseline: 0.55 p/L).
  • v1.0.0 corrects earlier forecasting shortcuts by limiting solar to daylight, capping impossible extra heater hours, and reporting a truer heat-up energy figure.
  • Exports are created locally in your browser and now include version and copyright details.
Quick start

1. Set your pool, heater, tariff, and cover assumptions.

2. Enter the current pool temperature and either type the next 3 days manually or fetch them from a UK postcode.

2b. Optionally set pool-in and pool-away dates plus your water rate to unlock a season cost summary.

3. Review the forecast, charts, maths checks, and exports.

Thermal model overview

This calculator models playpool heating using energy balance: heater input + solar gain − environmental heat loss. Outputs include monthly maintenance demand, target reachability, short-range forecast behaviour, and annual electricity cost for UK climate assumptions.

Public Heuristics Behind This Planner
  • US Department of Energy — Heating Your Pool: 26 °C / 78 °F is a common leisure comfort baseline.
  • US Department of Energy — Swimming Pool Covers: covers are commonly cited as reducing heating costs by roughly 50–70 % by suppressing evaporation.
  • Swim England and WHO recreational water guidance: children and learn-to-swim sessions are typically planned around about 28–30 °C.
  • Pool-owner forums and community discussions commonly report uncovered overnight drops of about 3–6 °C for small outdoor pools in cool or breezy conditions, with bubble covers sharply reducing that loss.

These public-query and forum-derived heuristics are used for planning prompts and cover assumptions; always treat them as indicative rather than guaranteed outcomes.
